Deepfake Voice Scams

Emerging

Overview

AI voice-cloning technology is used to impersonate a family member's voice in a distress call, demanding urgent money transfer.

How it works

Scammers gather short audio samples of a target's family member (often from social media videos) and use AI tools to clone their voice. A panicked call follows, claiming an accident, arrest, or kidnapping, demanding immediate payment.

Red flags to watch for

  • Extreme urgency paired with a request to keep the situation secret.
  • Requests for payment via unusual methods (crypto, gift cards, untraceable transfers).
  • Any inability to answer a personal verification question only the real person would know.

What to do if you're targeted

  • Hang up and call the family member back directly on their known number.
  • Establish a family "safe word" in advance for genuine emergencies.
  • Never send money based solely on a phone call without independent verification.
