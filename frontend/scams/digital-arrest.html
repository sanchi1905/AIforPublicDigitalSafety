Digital Arrest Scam

Still Active

Overview

Fraudsters impersonate police, CBI, ED, or customs officials and claim you're implicated in a crime (drug trafficking, money laundering, a parcel containing contraband). They create panic and isolate the victim to extract money.

How it works

A call or video call from someone in a fake uniform or office setup. They show fabricated arrest warrants or FIR documents. Victim is told to stay on video call continuously ("digital arrest") and not contact anyone, while being coerced into transferring money to "clear their name" or "verify funds are not black money."

Red flags to watch for

  • Real police never arrest or investigate over a phone/video call.
  • No legitimate agency asks for money to avoid arrest.
  • Being told to keep a call active and not tell family is a major warning sign.
  • Legitimate legal processes always involve written notices and physical appearance, not urgent video calls.

What to do if you're targeted

  • Hang up immediately.
  • Do not transfer any money.
  • Verify by calling the relevant department directly using numbers from their official website, not numbers given by the caller.
  • Report to 1930 or cybercrime.gov.in.
Cybercrime helpline: 1930 · cybercrime.gov.in
