Fake KYC Update

Still Active

Overview

Messages impersonating banks or UPI apps claiming your account will be blocked unless you "update your KYC" immediately via a link.

How it works

An SMS or WhatsApp message with urgent language and a shortened or lookalike link (e.g. sbi-kyc-verify.xyz). The link leads to a fake login page that captures your net banking credentials, or prompts you to "verify" by sharing an OTP.

Red flags to watch for

  • Banks do not ask you to update KYC via an SMS link.
  • URLs that don't match the bank's real domain.
  • Urgent countdown language ("blocked in 24 hours").
  • Requests for OTP, PIN, or CVV - banks never ask for these.

What to do if you're targeted

  • Never click links in unsolicited KYC messages.
  • Go directly to your bank's app or official website to check your KYC status.
  • Report the message and block the sender.
