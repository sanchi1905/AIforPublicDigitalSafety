Matrimonial / Romance Scams

Common

Overview

Fake profiles on matrimonial or dating platforms build an emotional relationship over weeks or months before requesting money.

How it works

The scammer builds trust through consistent messaging, often claiming to be based abroad or in the armed forces (limiting video calls/in-person meetings). Eventually, a fabricated crisis arises - a medical emergency, customs fee for a "gift," or travel costs to finally meet - requiring urgent money.

Red flags to watch for

  • Reluctance to video call or meet in person over an extended period.
  • A relationship that escalates to money requests within weeks.
  • Inconsistent details about their life or location.

What to do if you're targeted

  • Never send money to someone you haven't met in person.
  • Reverse image search their profile photos.
  • Report the profile to the platform and to cybercrime.gov.in if money was sent.
