SIM Swap Fraud

Still Active

Overview

Criminals obtain your personal details, then convince your telecom provider to issue a duplicate SIM card for your number, cutting off your real SIM.

How it works

Scammers gather your details via phishing or data leaks, then submit a SIM swap/replacement request to your telecom operator, sometimes with forged documents. Once the new SIM is active, they receive your OTPs and can access banking apps, email, and more.

Red flags to watch for

  • Your phone suddenly loses network/signal for an extended period with no explanation.
  • You receive an SMS about a SIM swap request you didn't make.
  • Unexpected "welcome" messages from your carrier for a new SIM.

What to do if you're targeted

  • If your phone unexpectedly loses signal for hours, contact your telecom provider immediately.
  • Enable SIM-lock/PIN features where available.
  • Monitor bank accounts for unauthorized access and report to 1930 immediately if a swap occurs.
Cybercrime helpline: 1930 · cybercrime.gov.in
