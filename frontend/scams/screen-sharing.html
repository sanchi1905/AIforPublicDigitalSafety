Screen-Sharing App Fraud

Still Active

Overview

Scammers pose as customer support (often after a fake "wrong refund" or "unauthorized transaction" claim) and convince victims to install a remote access app.

How it works

Caller asks you to install AnyDesk, TeamViewer, or similar, claiming they need to "reverse a refund" or "fix an issue." Once installed, they can see your screen and control your device, including opening your banking app and initiating transfers while you watch, sometimes even guiding you to enter your own PIN "to verify."

Red flags to watch for

  • No legitimate company asks you to install remote access software to fix a refund.
  • Any request to open your banking app during a support call is a major warning sign.

What to do if you're targeted

  • Never install remote access apps at the request of an unsolicited caller.
  • If you already have, uninstall immediately and change your banking passwords/PINs from a different, secure device.
  • Contact your bank to freeze the account if any transaction occurred.
