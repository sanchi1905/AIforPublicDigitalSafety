Electricity Bill Disconnection Scam

Still Active

Overview

Fake SMS messages threaten immediate power disconnection unless an "outstanding bill" is paid via a suspicious link, right now.

How it works

A message claims your electricity connection will be cut within hours due to unpaid bills, with a link to "pay immediately." The link leads to a fake payment page that captures card or UPI details.

Red flags to watch for

  • Real utility providers give advance written notice for disconnection, not same-day SMS threats.
  • Payment links that don't match your utility provider's official domain.

What to do if you're targeted

  • Check your bill status directly through your utility provider's official app or website, never through a link in an SMS.
  • Report the message and block the sender.
